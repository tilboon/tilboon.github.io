Biography

I’m a Ph.D. student in Electrical & Computer Engineering at UCLA (GPA 3.9), focused on low-power autonomous robotics and embedded systems. My work spans microrobot autonomy, swarm navigation, on-device sensing, and robust control/estimation for resource-constrained platforms.

I’m a Graduate Research Assistant in the LEMUR Lab (Advisor: Ankur Mehta), working on hardware/software co-design for small-scale, battery-free robots, including state estimation (e.g., Kalman filtering) and communication/control algorithms for swarm navigation.

Research & Experience

LEMUR Lab (UCLA) — Battery-free autonomy & swarm navigation

Hardware/software development for low-power, small-scale, battery-free autonomous robots. Includes estimation-driven control (Kalman filtering) and communication/control algorithms for swarm navigation.

Lab · Project page

MIT Lincoln Laboratory — Thermal detection & closed-loop UAV guidance

Built an end-to-end vision/control stack for real-time thermal detection and autonomous UAV guidance. Partnered with ModalAI to extend platform capabilities and transitioned the system to ARL.

Overview · Demo

Iyer Lab (UW) — Battery-free origami gliding sensor nodes

Designed embedded, battery-free wireless gliding sensor nodes with origami body triggered by wireless actuators; implemented low-power wireless data collection on Nordic nRF52 platforms.

Project · Related publication

Reality Lab (UW) — VR interaction research (Unity)

Developed a VR system to study human interaction with alternate species (Giant Pacific Octopus), including data collection and physics-based mapping of human motion to a simulated model using Unity.

Video

UW CubeSat SOC-I — Secure communications (NASA Space Grant)

Built message encryption/validation via HMAC and configured data packet/protocol setup for uplink and downlink communications.
